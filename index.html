Risk Discovery & Prioritization

A structured approach to identifying, assessing, and monitoring risk across the money movement portfolio

Objective

This framework provides a standardized methodology for proactively identifying, categorizing, and ranking emerging operational risks across multiple business lines. It enables portfolio-level visibility for executive leadership by quantifying both inherent risk exposure and the mitigating effect of existing controls — resulting in a residual risk view that drives prioritized action. The scoring methodology uses criteria across five risk dimensions (Financial, Operational, Regulatory, Reputational, and Third-Party) alongside Control Maturity to ensure risks are assessed with consideration of control process effectiveness, and residual risks are prioritized accordingly.

Step 1

Inherent Risk Assessment

Score each risk on a 5×5 grid of Likelihood × Impact before any controls are applied. This produces an inherent risk score (1–25) representing the raw exposure if no mitigation existed.

➔
Step 2

Residual Risk Calculation

Overlay a Control Maturity assessment (1=Optimized to 5=Initial) onto the inherent score. Residual Risk = Inherent Score × Control Maturity, revealing what risk remains after existing controls are applied.

➔
Step 3

Prioritized Action

Map the final residual score to five action tiers (Critical → Minimal), each with a defined response: escalation cadence, ownership level, and review frequency — enabling executives to allocate resources where they matter most.

Step 2Residual Risk Heat Map

How It Works

Residual Risk Score = Inherent Risk Score × Control Maturity Level

A risk that is Critical (5) on an inherent basis but has Optimized (1) controls scores 5 — effectively Minimal residual risk.

Conversely, a High (4) inherent risk with Initial (5) controls scores 20 — High residual risk, requiring urgent investment in controls.

I N H E R I T  R I S K  S C O R E
C O N T R O L  M A T U R I T Y
Control Maturity considers the maturity and mitigating effectiveness of controls and other risk management processes as designed. Ratings should be validated with cross-functional stakeholders (Risk Policy, Operations, Compliance, Internal Audit) to ensure alignment.

Governance & Strategic Alignment

Cross-Functional Alignment

This risk prioritization framework must be developed and validated collaboratively across key stakeholder groups — including Risk Policy, Risk Operations, Risk Analytics, Compliance, Internal Audit, and the Chief Accounting Office (CAO).

Company-Wide Strategy & Direction

Risk prioritization does not exist in isolation — it must be aligned with the company's broader strategic direction and business objectives. As the organization evolves (entering new markets, launching new products, scaling existing operations), the risk landscape shifts accordingly. This framework should be reviewed periodically to ensure that risk appetite thresholds, impact criteria, and monitoring priorities reflect current corporate strategy and executive leadership's direction for the business.

Monthly Monitoring & Governance

A repeatable, audit-defensible QA cycle that tracks accuracy and timeliness of case decisions, identifies root causes, and closes the loop through coaching, SOP updates, and escalation.

📋 Queue Example: Regular Funding
📌 Monthly QA Process — Overview & Flow 4 Stages

Each month, a QA sample is pulled from iBoss for the Regular Funding queue. The reviewer independently re-evaluates each case against the SOP — without looking at the analyst's decision first — then compares the outcome. The score, root cause, and actions are documented in a Google Sheet and summarised in a QA memo to Ops Leadership.

🤖 Bot & AI Agent QA — Separate Monitoring Track

RPA bot and AI agent decisioning quality is monitored separately from human QA scores. They are different control populations with different error profiles and different remediation paths.

🔍 How Bot QA Works

Same independent-decision methodology applied to a sample of bot-decisioned cases each month
Bot QA score tracked separately — never averaged into human QA score
Accuracy drop → escalated to RPA team, not Ops. Coaching is not the remediation path
Bot findings documented in a dedicated section of the QA Summary Memo with specific case IDs and error pattern
